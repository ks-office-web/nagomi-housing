Assessment Perspective

How we see this property.

K's Office — Independent Judgment

This is not a property valued primarily for its surface yield. Its value proposition is built on a combination of rarity, scale, privacy, coastal positioning, and the kind of ownership satisfaction that is difficult to replicate in Fukuoka's current market.

The top-floor location, the ocean views, the tea room, and the low-density floor plan are genuinely uncommon features — not marketing language. Buyers who understand these elements intuitively will recognise this immediately. Buyers seeking modern efficiency may not.

The building was completed in 1993, which means a buyer needs to be clear-eyed about building age, management quality, and ongoing holding costs. These are not reasons to avoid the property — but they are things that must be properly verified before committing.

In our view, this property suits a buyer who has already moved past the question of yield and is asking a different question: what kind of place do I want to own in Japan, and for how long?

Before Moving Forward

What should be carefully verified.

  • 01 Ongoing ownership costs in full — management fee, reserve fund, parking, and total annual holding burden
  • 02 Building condition and management quality — including long-term repair plans and reserve fund adequacy
  • 03 Suitability for the buyer's intended purpose — primary residence, second home, or other use
  • 04 Long-term resale positioning — for a rare, high-end property of this age and character, this requires local judgment, not assumption
  • 05 The balance between rarity and efficiency — whether the buyer's priorities genuinely align with what this property offers
